The Status Quo

The ocean is in crisis.
We are still flying blind.

Plastic pollution accelerates. Ocean dead zones expand. Contamination spreads beyond any single nation's jurisdiction. Yet the systems we rely on to monitor these threats are decades behind the speed and scale of the damage. We have the data. We have never had the intelligence to act on it.

What We Do

Three layers of ocean intelligence.

01

Detect

Identify plastic accumulation zones, chemical plumes, and biological stress indicators from satellite imagery in near real-time and at planetary scale.

02

Map

Build high-resolution pollution maps showing where contamination originated, where it is drifting, and where it will concentrate next.

03

Predict

Model ocean current dynamics and pollution trajectories so cleanup and policy efforts stay ahead of the crisis rather than react to it.

Our Mission

We build AI systems that help humanity
understand and restore Earth's ocean.

We believe the people closest to the problem should shape the intelligence that guides the solution.

Starting with the ocean, the planet's largest and least understood ecosystem. BlueMind fuses satellite imagery, oceanographic data streams, and machine learning to create the first real-time intelligence layer for ocean health.

"Starting with the ocean. Expanding to everything."

Our Science

Satellite eyes.
Ocean intelligence.

We combine multi-spectral satellite imagery, ocean current modeling, and deep learning to detect, classify, and track pollution at a scale no vessel or drone could reach. Our systems learn from the ocean itself, continuously refining their understanding as new data streams in.

This is not monitoring. It is a living intelligence system for the ocean.

Predicted Impact

What becomes possible
when we can see.

We are at the beginning. But the implications of real-time ocean intelligence are profound, for conservation, for policy, and for the communities whose survival depends on a healthy sea.

When governments know exactly where to deploy cleanup fleets, waste stops accumulating faster than it is removed. When corporations can see the full downstream path of their discharge, accountability becomes undeniable. When the ocean can speak, someone will finally have to listen.
